# Acme Corp - Annual Financial Report 2025

## Executive Summary
Acme Corp has experienced a year of aggressive expansion and mixed financial outcomes. The organization successfully captured 15% more market share in the North American logistics sector. However, this growth has come at the expense of a tightened balance sheet.

## Q3 Revenue and Earnings
During the third quarter (Q3), Acme Corp reported total revenue of $45.2 million, a 12% increase year-over-year. Net income for the quarter stood at $4.1 million. The primary driver for this growth was the successful launch of our automated supply chain services, which accounted for 40% of the quarter's total revenue.

## Debt Obligations and Equity
Total outstanding liabilities at the close of Q3 were $125 million, compared to $85 million in the previous fiscal year. Total shareholder equity is currently valued at $60 million.

## Financial Risk and High Debt Ratio
The most significant financial risk currently facing Acme Corp is related to our high debt ratio. Our debt-to-equity ratio currently stands at an alarming 2.08. This elevated leverage was necessitated by the $40 million acquisition of 'Global Freight Tech' in Q2. 

If interest rates from the Federal Reserve increase by more than 50 basis points in the upcoming quarter, our debt servicing costs could increase by an estimated $1.2 million annually. This high debt ratio severely restricts our borrowing capacity for future emergency capital and poses a liquidity risk if Q4 revenue targets are not met.

## Forward Outlook
To mitigate the risks associated with the high debt ratio, the board has approved a pause on all new acquisitions for the next 18 months. Instead, leadership will focus exclusively on optimizing the profitability of the existing technological assets and reducing operating expenses by 8% across all departments.
